The following are important while you are doing Audit

 

 

BE CLEAN BE HEALTHY

 

 

Q. Are members of management aware of the associate illness guidelines? 

 

Check the management awareness on five big food borne illness and what action to be taken incase any food handlers reported with five big food borne illness.

Q. Are all food handling associates free of food borne illness symptoms?

Visually observe the associates. Any associate handling food is observed with symptoms five big food borne illness and also have symptoms associated with GI illness.

Q. Are all the food handlers undergone the medical check-up?

Verify the medical record file and incase reports are not available but undergone medical checkup then don't deduct marks but record in report.

Q. Are food handlers’ hands and wrists free of cuts or sores and properly bandaged and covered? 

Observation

Q. Are designated hand wash sinks properly supplied & maintained?

Check the Hand wash station set up compliance. A compliance Hand wash station must have Water, approved soap, tissue paper, dustbin, wall mounted hand wash SOP. Water, soap and dustbin is minimum requirement (Rest Room) but in fresh/preparation area eg. Meat, bakery  must have tissue paper also. Partial compliance if wall mounted, SOP or Approved soap is missing.

Q. Are food handlers washing hands appropriately and/or properly? 

Observe associates are washing hands. While hand wash demonstration, associate hands must not touch the sink and tap. All five steps of hand washing are demonstrated. Proper Soap should be taken in hand and min 20-30sec contact time with sufficient lather formation.

Q. Are food work areas free of personal items and is there no evidence of food, drink or tobacco usage? 

Look for evidence in the section or in the associate’s pocket.

Q. Are food handlers following the dress code policy in the food work areas ? 

Food handlers must wear clean white chef coat and hair guard. Other associates including store management must wear blue coat.

Q. Are food handlers following the Jewelry policy in the food work areas?  

Jewelry is not allowed in preparation area except single finger ring. For details please go through the skill card.

Q. Are food handlers preventing bare hand contact with ready to eat foods? 

Observe and also check the understanding by giving some situation. This is applicable for unpackaged foods.

Q. Are food handling associates following personal hygiene standards?

Personal hygiene is critical for Meat, Bakery and cut fruits & vegetables processing section. Trimmed nail, trimmed hair, shaved, etc. Overall grooming standards for associates handling packaged food, Fruits and vegetable, etc, including house keeping associates.

 

KEEP IT COLD KEEP IT HOT

 

Q. Are chilled and frozen foods received within temperature guidelines?

Chilled foods: 0C to 5C or label description whichever is higher, Frozen foods:  -18C or below or label description whichever is higher. Check the cold chain receiving logs.

Don't mark non-compliance if corrective action recorded in case of temperature deviation.

Management must verify the logs on daily basis.

Cross check the temperature, If any chilled & Frozen received during the audit.

Q. Are associates aware of cold chain break guidelines?

Cold chain break should not be more than 30 min. Applicable during receiving, processing, handling, etc.

Q. Are all cold held potentially hazardous foods kept at or below 5° C ? 

Chilled foods: 0C to 5C or label description whichever is higher. Check product temperature in individual chiller. Sampling size - 3 samples per chiller. (If product temperature of two samples less than 5.1C or within label declaration then compliance, if two sample within range of 5.1 to 7C then partial compliance, if there is no majority then go for 5 samples per chiller and consider majority)

 

Q. Are correct storage practice being followed in chillers? 

Check load line are followed in individual chillers. Proper storage practices are followed to ensure no blockage in air circulation. (e.g., In case of vertical chiller - gaps in back side, lower shelves available, top suction fans are not blocked with merchandize, etc)

Q. Are frozen foods kept at or below -18° C? 

Frozen foods: less than or equal to -18C or label description whichever is higher. Check product temperature in individual freezer . Sampling size - 3 samples per freezer . (If product temperature of two samples less than -18C or within label declaration then compliance, if two sample within range of -18 to -15C then partial compliance, if there is no majority then go for 5 samples per freezer and consider majority)

Q. Are correct storage practice being followed in freezers? 

Check load line are followed in individual chillers. Proper storage practices are followed to ensure no blockage in air circulation. (e.g., In case of vertical chiller - gaps in back side, lower shelves available, top suction fans are not blocked with merchandize, etc)

Q. Are all hot held potentially hazardous foods kept at or above 60°C? 

Product temperature in hot case must be at ot above 60C. Check the date code stickers on individual tray and also hot hold temperature log. Don't mark non-compliance if 2hrs shelf life guidelines is in practice incase product temperature less than 60C.

Q. Are thermometers in working condition and validated within defined frequency?

Only working thermometer is to be check for validation and also check validation log. Frequency for thermometer validation is once in a week for small stores and daily for CHM

Q. Are temperature of all the chillers and freezers monitored within defined frequency ?

Every two hrs. temperature is be monitor for all the chiller and freezer. Check the record compliance. Check the management verification on daily basis.

 

WASH RINSE SANITIZE

 

Q. Are floor, vernicle and overhead structure clean?

Overall cleaning and hygiene, no off or foul smell, no cob webs, damaged/poor appearance wall mount poster, tube light, AC duct, etc

Q. Are chiller and freezer cleaned?

No foul smell inside, no old food particles deposition, check gasket, fans, doors, etc

Q. Are merchandising shelves, fixture,etc clean? 

Dust deposition, old food product deposition, dead or live insect, fungal growth, etc.

Q. Are drains clean and free flowing and no stagnant standing water?  

No foul smell inside, no blockage, etc. If stagnant water is fresh then don't mark non-compliance.

Q. Are food contact surfaces of equipment and utensils clean?   

Observe the equipment/utensils those are not in use.

Q. Are food contact surface properly sanitized and sanitizer at the correct concentration?

Optimum concentration of sanitizer is 200 to 400ppm. Oasis 146 Multi-Quat Sanitizer is the approved sanitizer. The concentration of sanitizer is to be check with QT40 test strips. Dip the QT40 strips for 10 second in the solution and compare the colour with standard colour chart.

Q. Are approved cleaning chemicals and tools used and readily available?  

Approved cleaning chemicals -

1. All Purpose Cleaner - detergent for cleaning of any surface including food contact

2. Oasis 146 Multi-Quat Sanitizer - Sanitizer for food contact surface after cleaning with detergent

3. Clean Hands - hand washing

4. Kool Klene - Chiller and freezer cleaning

5. QT40 Test strips - To check sanitizer concentration

6. Grill Cleaner - Carbon removal from grill, oven, etc

7. Oasis Pro - Toilet Bowl Cleaner

Q. Are all chemicals properly labeled and authorized? 

All bottle must have label and in good condition. Missing of label in any one of the bottle is to be consider non-compliance.

Q. Are cleaning checklists maintained? 

Applicable to Only Meat, Bakery and cut fruits & vegetable processing area; Cleaning logs are maintained and up to date.

 

DON'T CROSS CONTAMINATE

 

Q. Are raw foods kept separated from Reedy to Eat foods and separated by species? 

Raw food & Ready to Eat food are stored separately.  In case of emergency RTE should come on the top shelf of chiller than Fish, below that Mutton & in Last or at bottom Raw chicken would come.

Q. Are chemicals stored properly to prevent contamination? 

Housekeeping chemicals should be stored in the chemical storage cup board or designated storage area.

 

Q. Is there any evidence of physical hazard contamination in food?

Observe any evidence of physical hazard in food.

Q. Are the food packaging materials of food grade?

Food packaging materials that are use in fresh department to pack open food. Poly bag with 40 micron mentioned on bag and paper bag with aluminum liner inside is permissible.

Ask for sourcing of bags - if local purchase then put comments but no deduction on mark.

Q. Are food and non-food separated? 

Non-food such as house hold insecticide, merchandise Chemicals should not be stored above food/primary food packaging materials and along the food/primary food packaging materials.

Check in the back room, Raiser, etc.

Q. Are veg and non-veg food stored separately? 

Observe in chilled and frozen food or any other food.

Q. Are all utensils, food packaging materials, etc properly stored?  

Check the condensation or water leakages from chiller and AC unit. If water falls on open food or damaging packaging materials then no mark.

Q. Are food areas free of condensation and water leakage? 

Check the condensation or water leakages from chiller and AC unit. If water falls on open food or damaging packaging materials then no mark.

Q. Are all the food contact surfaces non-corrosive, on-damaged, etc. ?  

Chopper with wooden handle or chopping board surface is not uniform. Rusting on Knife holder. Cutting tools damage, etc.

Q. Is the water storage tank cleaned  periodically and secured?  Are records being maintained in the register?  

Is the tank under lock and key, visually cleaned including intermediate tank if any. Min cleaning frequency is quarterly.

Q. Are food items stored at a sufficient distance away from the walls and floor? 

 

COOK IT CHILL IT

 

Food items should be kept 6 inch above floor. Don’t mark non-compliance if kept temporarily or work in process.

Q. Are foods cooked to correct internal temperature?  

Internal cooking temperature is more than 74C. Check the cooking logs and observe incase of any cooking activity during audit

Q. Are prepared foods reheated at recommended temperature?

Check the reheating logs and observe in case of any reheating activity during audit

Q. Are store following the cooking oil changing guidelines?  

Check knowledge on SOP and condition of oil visually.

Q. Are proper thawing procedures of potentially hazardous food (PHF) followed?  

Check knowledge, thawing at chiller only. Check the availability of facility

Q. Are proper cooling of hot potentially hazardous food (PHF) items executed? 

Check knowledge. Food once cooked should be brought down from 60oC to 21oC within 2 hours and to 5oC within 6 hours.

Q. Are relevant log books maintained and verified by management?

Check records

 

PRODUCT QUALITY & SAFETY

 

Q. Are store using approved ingredients during preparation of food and within recommended guidelines?  

Approved ingredients for marination in meat is BSI premix

In live bakery ask for list of approved ingredients and check the compliance

Q. Are packaged foods within used by date/BBD/Expiry? 

Random sampling in every category/section

Clearance section must be checked short shelf life and slow moving items on priority, Check cold drinks, muffins, eggs, etc.

Q. Are food or food ingredients dated & within used by date?  

To be check in open food and/or in-house production/processing area.

Standard date code sticker should be pasted

Check the date code of few tray of chicken/Mutton with cold chain receiving log book

Q. Are FEFO and Rotation being followed?   

Replenishment from Back room/Raiser to shelves,

Progressive Mark down is evidence in product less than one month available shelf life.

Clearance section should not have products having expiry in next 3 days(if it is with cashier then don't mark non-compliance).

WIBI (Would I Buy It) in practice in fresh fruits and vegetable section

Check perishable checklist and section management documents compliance, if no documents maintained then 100% non-compliance

Q. Are foods on display wholesome and free of insects, mould and spoilage?

Check for insect infestation on staples, puffed packet, fungus, etc, on the product kept on sales floor/POS (Don't mark non-compliance for up to 3 packets in a single SKU. Mark non-compliance if more than 5 SKU)

Check fruits and vegetable overall quality on display

Q. Are packaged food kept on shelves not damaged, poor package appearance,etc    

Check in clip strip, clearance area, modular, Bins, sales floor, etc

Q. Are all non-saleable food stored correctly and disposed off from depts on daily basis?
